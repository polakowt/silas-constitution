# Decision: First Public Post About the Constitution

**Date:** 2026-02-20
**Decided by:** Both (Silas proposed, Tom approved)
**Constitutional Principle:** Foundation III — Transparency, Humility, and Human Partnership

## Context

The constitution was drafted and the repo was taking shape. The question arose: when do we start talking about this publicly? There's value in sharing early (feedback, accountability, community) but also risk (half-baked ideas getting locked in, unwanted attention before we're ready).

## Options Considered

1. **Post immediately on LinkedIn and X** — Maximum visibility, establishes priority
   - Pros: Gets feedback early, builds momentum
   - Cons: Constitution still in draft, might attract premature criticism

2. **Wait until v1.0 is "done"** — Ship when polished
   - Pros: Stronger first impression
   - Cons: "Done" is a moving target, delays accountability

3. **Soft launch on Moltbook, then LinkedIn** — Test with AI community first
   - Pros: Get feedback from people who understand the space, iterate, then go wider
   - Cons: Smaller initial audience

## Decision

Option 3. Post on Moltbook first for community feedback, then write a LinkedIn article once we've incorporated initial reactions. Silas drafts, Tom reviews and posts (his voice on public platforms — per autonomy scope).

## Reasoning

- Foundation III says welcome oversight — sharing early is living that principle
- Principle 5 (shrewd as serpents) says test with a friendly audience before going broad
- Posting as Tom (not Silas) respects the boundary that external comms in his voice require his approval
- Moltbook community understands AI agent development and can give substantive feedback

## Outcome

Moltbook post received thoughtful engagement. Several community members raised questions about the autonomy model that led to clarifying Level 3 vs Level 4 boundaries. LinkedIn article scheduled for the following week with improvements incorporated.

---

*Transparency means sharing the process, not just the product.*
